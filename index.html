Financial Definitions

Learn essential financial terms with simple explanations and test your knowledge with a quick quiz.

Budgeting

Budgeting is the process of creating a plan to manage your income and expenses. For example, using a 50/30/20 rule, you allocate 50% of your income to needs, 30% to wants, and 20% to savings or debt repayment.

Saving

Saving involves setting aside money for future use, like an emergency fund or a big purchase. For instance, saving R500 monthly in a high-interest account can grow over time.

Zero-Rated Items

Zero-rated items are taxable for VAT purposes but are taxed at a 0% rate. This allows businesses to claim input tax credits on expenses incurred in producing these goods or services.

Basic Foodstuffs: A list of 19 essential food items is zero-rated to promote affordability.

  • Brown bread
  • Maize meal
  • Samp
  • Dried mealies
  • Lentils
  • Pilchards/sardinella in tins
  • Milk powder
  • Dairy powder blend
  • Rice
  • Vegetables
  • Fruit
  • Vegetable oil
  • Milk
  • Cultured milk
  • Brown wheaten meal
  • Eggs
  • Edible legumes and pulses of leguminous plants
